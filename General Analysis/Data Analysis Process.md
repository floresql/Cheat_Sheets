# Data Analysis Process — Google's 6-Phase Playbook

## 1. Ask
**Goal:** Define the problem to be solved and understand stakeholder expectations.
*   **Define the Problem:** Identify the gap between the current state and the ideal state.
*   **SMART Questions:** Use Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Relevant, and Time-bound questions to focus the scope.
*   **Stakeholder Engagement:** Collaborate with leaders to ensure the analysis aligns with business goals.

## 2. Prepare
**Goal:** Determine what data is needed to solve the problem and how to collect it.
*   **Data Sourcing:** Identify internal (databases, CRM) and external (APIs, public datasets) sources.
*   **Data Integrity:** Ensure the data is objective, unbiased, and credible.
*   **Organization:** Decide how to store and protect the data while planning the research timeline.

## 3. Process
**Goal:** Clean and transform raw data to ensure it is accurate and consistent.
*   **Data Cleaning:** Remove duplicates, fix typos, and handle missing or inconsistent values.
*   **Verification:** Check the cleaned data against stakeholders' expectations for correctness and completeness.
*   **Bias Check:** Identify and mitigate any biases that could lead to unrepresentative results.

## 4. Analyze
**Goal:** Transform and organize information to find patterns, relationships, and trends.
*   **Calculation:** Perform necessary calculations and aggregations to uncover insights.
*   **Storytelling:** Use tools like SQL or spreadsheets to "let the data tell its story" and solve the business problem.
*   **Substantiation:** Ensure findings are supported by evidence rather than gut instinct.

## 5. Share
**Goal:** Interpret and present findings to stakeholders to enable informed decision-making.
*   **Visualization:** Create engaging charts and dashboards (using tools like Tableau or Excel) to make complex data understandable.
*   **Stakeholder Feedback:** Use presentations as an opportunity to get different views and improve the final result.
*   **Design Thinking:** Empathize with the audience to ensure the story is easy to follow.

## 6. Act
**Goal:** Use the analytical insights to implement solutions and solve the original business problem.
*   **Data-Driven Decisions:** Provide recommendations that allow stakeholders to take action based on facts.
*   **Implementation:** Work with company leaders to execute changes based on the findings.
*   **Iteration:** Use feedback to refine future projects and continuously improve business outcomes.
